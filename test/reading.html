1.Global warming is a pressing environmental issue that has far-reaching consequences for our planet. One of the most concerning effects of global warming is the rise in average temperatures across the Earth's surface. This increase in temperature can lead to the melting of polar ice caps and glaciers, contributing to rising sea levels. Consequently, low-lying coastal regions face the threat of inundation, displacing millions of people and causing the loss of valuable coastal ecosystems. Furthermore, global warming can disrupt weather patterns, leading to more frequent and severe weather events such as hurricanes, droughts, and heatwaves. These changes have a profound impact on agriculture, water resources, and ecosystems, affecting both human livelihoods and biodiversity.
